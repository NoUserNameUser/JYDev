# Personal Homepage Redesign Research

Date: 2026-04-28

## Sources Reviewed

- Bruno Simon portfolio: https://bruno-simon.com/
- Brittany Chiang portfolio: https://brittanychiang.com/
- Guillermo Rauch personal site: https://rauchg.com/
- Codesi personal website examples: https://codesi.ai/blog/best-personal-websites/
- Awwwards interactive portfolio examples: https://www.awwwards.com/websites/interactive-portfolio-examples/

## Useful Patterns

1. Play can be the proof.
   Bruno Simon turns the portfolio itself into an interactive world. The important lesson is not to copy the whole 3D game, but to make the interaction demonstrate the maker's skill.

2. Clarity still wins.
   Brittany Chiang and Guillermo Rauch use strong information hierarchy, short positioning, and obvious navigation. The homepage should say who Jackie is, what he builds, and how to contact him within the first viewport.

3. Interaction should support the content.
   The best examples use motion, hover states, and transitions to make the page feel responsive. They avoid hiding core information behind novelty.

4. Personal tone matters.
   Strong portfolios feel authored. The copy should sound direct, human, and specific rather than generic developer marketing.

5. Mobile-first and lightweight performance are part of the brand.
   Heavy effects can hurt credibility if they make the site slow. Canvas interaction is a better fit here than a large 3D engine.

## Design Direction Applied

- Replaced parchment/gold styling with a darker product-playground palette: ink, mint, coral, acid green, and warm yellow.
- Replaced the old scroll-pinned hero with an immediate interactive canvas hero.
- Added mode buttons in the hero so the visitor can change the page's active "build mode".
- Reframed services as "Build Modes" and the timeline as "Process" to avoid generic resume-template energy.
- Removed fake-feeling placeholders from the profile area and turned it into a graphic identity board.
- Kept contact and social links obvious, because play should not make the site harder to use.
